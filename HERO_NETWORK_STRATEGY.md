## The Opportunity

Nearly all video marketing content on the web targets **the business owner** deciding to "start doing video." Almost nothing is written for **the agency professional** whose job it is to deliver video for clients — and who faces completely different problems: site visit logistics, client footage bottlenecks, retainer scalability, multi-client management.

That's our unfair advantage. HubSpot, Buffer, Wistia, and Descript are all writing for the end business owner. We write for the person producing video on their behalf.

---

## Core Content Thesis

**The agency video production workflow is broken at the capture stage, not the editing stage.**

Every article we write connects back to this thesis in some way. Remote capture, client briefing, retainer scaling, tool stack — it all flows from the same insight: the bottleneck is getting usable footage from clients, not editing it once you have it.

## Content Principles

### 1. Write what competitors can't
HubSpot and Buffer write for business owners. Only Hero Network can credibly write for agency producers from the perspective of a tool built specifically for that workflow. Every article should have at least one angle that only we can take honestly.

### 2. Operational specificity beats marketing theory
The reader is a working professional. They don't need convincing that video matters. They need operational help: how to brief clients, how to manage the queue, how to price the retainer. Get specific.

### 3. Long-tail first
No domain authority yet. "Video production" is unwinnable. "How to get clients to record video for your agency" is not. Start with specificity and build toward broader terms.

### 4. Depth over volume
10 comprehensive 2,500-word articles outperform 50 thin 500-word posts. Google rewards expertise signals. We're building a content moat, not a content farm.

### 5. One CTA per article
Every article ends with a single link to Founder Pricing or the main site. Stack framing: "Add it to your production stack — $79/mo."

### 6. Internal linking from day one
As articles publish, link between them immediately. Pillar articles accumulate links from every cluster article. Update `context/internal-links-map.md` with each new URL.

## Notes on Domain Authority

heronetwork.io is a new domain for content. Realistically:

- Months 1–2: Indexing and early crawling — minimal visible traffic
- Month 3: First rankings appear, mostly long-tail
- Month 4–5: Compounding begins — rankings improve, more terms indexed
- Month 6+: Meaningful traffic if publishing cadence held

**The mistake to avoid**: Publishing 3 articles and checking rankings after 2 weeks. SEO is a 6-month minimum commitment. The cadence in Phase 1 is what determines whether Month 5 looks good.
