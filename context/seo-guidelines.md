# Hero Network SEO Guidelines

SEO requirements and standards for all blog content. These apply to every article published on heronetwork.io.

---

## Content Length Requirements

- **Minimum**: 2,000 words
- **Target**: 2,500–3,000 words for informational content
- **Competitive articles**: Match or exceed top 3 SERP results (use /research to check)
- **Short-form exception**: FAQ pages, landing pages, and comparison pages may be shorter — quality over length
- **Note**: Hero Network has zero existing blog content. Focus on comprehensive first-content pieces that establish topical authority from scratch.

---

## Keyword Optimization Rules

### Keyword Placement
- **H1**: Must include the primary keyword
- **First 100 words**: Primary keyword must appear naturally in the opening
- **H2s**: Include primary keyword in at least 2-3 subheadings
- **Image alt text**: Include keyword where naturally relevant
- **URL slug**: Include primary keyword

### Keyword Density
- **Primary keyword**: 1–2% density (approximately 20-40 uses in a 2,500-word article)
- **Secondary keywords**: Include naturally throughout, no specific density target
- **LSI/Semantic keywords**: Use topic-related terms to signal content depth
- **Warning**: If it sounds forced when read aloud, reduce density

### Keyword Stuffing Red Flags
- Same phrase repeated verbatim in consecutive sentences
- Unnatural anchor text variations just to include keyword
- Keyword in every H2 regardless of relevance
- Awkward phrase construction to fit keyword

## Meta Element Standards

### Meta Title
- **Length**: 50-60 characters (hard limit — Google truncates beyond 60)
- **Format**: Primary keyword | Year or modifier if valuable
- **Sentence case** (not Title Case for Hero Network)
- **Include a differentiation signal**: "Without a Crew", "For Small Business", "That Actually Works"

**Template options**:
- `[Primary Keyword]: [Specific Benefit] ([Year])`
- `How to [Action] Without [Pain Point]`
- `[Number] Ways to [Achieve Outcome] for [Audience]`

### Meta Description
- **Length**: 150-160 characters (hard limit)
- **Include**: Primary keyword, a secondary keyword, and a compelling reason to click
- **Action-oriented**: Tell them what they'll get
- **No duplicate descriptions**: Every article needs unique meta description
- **Avoid**: "In this article we discuss..." — tell them the value, not the format

## Link Strategy Guidelines

### Internal Links
- **Minimum**: 3 internal links per article
- **Maximum**: 5-6 (don't over-link)
- **Priority targets**: Pricing page, pillar articles, closely related cluster articles
- **Placement**: Contextually relevant — link when it genuinely helps the reader
- **Anchor text**: Descriptive, keyword-rich, but natural
- **No exact-match keyword stuffing in anchors**: Vary the anchor text

### External Links
- **Minimum**: 2 external authority links per article
- **Types**: Industry statistics, research studies, reputable publications
- **Quality check**: DA 50+ preferred; no spammy or thin sites
- **Nofollow if needed**: Affiliate links, sponsored content, or low-authority sites
- **Preferred sources**: HubSpot Research, Wyzowl Video Stats, LinkedIn Marketing, Think With Google

## Readability Requirements

### Reading Level
- **Target**: 8th-10th grade reading level (Flesch-Kincaid Grade Level 8-10)
- **Flesch Reading Ease**: 50-70 (fairly easy to read)
- **Why**: Our audience is business owners, not academics — plain language builds trust

### Sentence and Paragraph Structure
- **Average sentence**: 15-20 words
- **Paragraphs**: 2-4 sentences max in blog content
- **Subheadings**: Every 300-400 words to aid scannability
- **Passive voice**: Max 10% of sentences — flag for rewrite
- **Transition words**: Use but don't overuse — helps readability signals

## Content Structure Requirements

### Introduction (First 150 words)
- **Hook**: Open with a specific observation, statistic, or scenario the reader recognizes
- **Problem identification**: Name the friction or challenge explicitly
- **Promise**: Tell them what they'll learn or what outcome they'll get
- **Primary keyword**: Must appear in first 100 words
- **No**: "In this article, I'm going to explain..." — just do it

### Body Structure
- **H1**: One, contains primary keyword, benefit-focused
- **H2 sections**: 4-8 major sections, each substantial (200-400 words)
- **H3 subsections**: Use when a section has multiple distinct sub-points
- **Evidence**: Each major claim needs a stat, example, or specific scenario
- **Examples**: Minimum 2 concrete examples per article, ideally more

### Conclusion
- **Summarize the key insight** (not a bullet list of everything you said)
- **CTA**: One specific call to action — link to Hero Network pricing or related article
- **No "In conclusion..."**: Just write the conclusion
- **Final word count**: Conclusion should be 100-200 words

## Featured Snippet Optimization

For informational keywords, optimize for featured snippets:

### "What is" queries
- Include a clear definition in the first H2 or as a bolded paragraph
- Keep definition to 40-60 words

### "How to" queries  
- Use numbered steps in H3 format
- Each step: action verb + object + context
- Example: "1. Open the camera app and set it to video mode"

### "Best X" / List queries
- Use H3 for each item
- Keep list items concise and scannable
- Include a brief description under each item

## Content Quality Standards

### Each Article Must
- [ ] Provide unique value vs. top 3 SERP competitors
- [ ] Be factually accurate (verify statistics before citing)
- [ ] Include actionable advice — not just theoretical
- [ ] Be comprehensive for the topic (don't leave major questions unanswered)
- [ ] Sound like a knowledgeable human wrote it (pass the read-aloud test)
- [ ] Have a clear thesis that the whole article supports

### Each Article Must NOT
- [ ] Recycle generic advice that's on every other site
- [ ] Make claims without supporting evidence
- [ ] Bury the main point to pad word count
- [ ] Include AI-signature phrases (delve, crucial, tapestry, etc.)
- [ ] Repeat the same point in different words to hit word count
- [ ] End with "If you found this helpful, share it!" or similar filler
