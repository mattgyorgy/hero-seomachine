## What's Included in Editing

Every edit submitted through Hero Network includes the following production capabilities:

### Core Editing
- **Cuts and pacing** — removing dead air, tightening transitions, structuring the content for flow
- **Transitions** — professional transitions between segments and scenes
- **Audio cleanup** — background noise reduction, level normalization, consistent audio quality throughout
- **Color correction** — basic color grading and correction for a polished, professional look

### Enhanced Production
- **Caption burn-in** — hardcoded captions/subtitles rendered directly onto the video
- **Lower thirds and graphics** — name titles, branded graphics, and on-screen text elements
- **B-roll insertion** — supplementary footage pulled from the project assets and the Client's Smart Library (brand assets, logos, previously uploaded media)

### Custom Deliverables
Hosts specify exactly what they need for each project. Deliverables are fully customizable — common formats include:
- Full-length episodes
- Social media clips (optimized for platform dimensions)
- Vertical shorts (for TikTok, Instagram Reels, YouTube Shorts)
- Highlight reels
- Any custom combination the Host requests

### File Delivery
- **Standard format:** MP4
- **Rush turnaround:** Available for expedited delivery at additional cost
- **Standard turnaround:** 3–5 business days

## What's NOT Included

The following are outside the scope of standard editing:

- Custom motion graphics
- Animation
- Visual effects (VFX)

These exclusions exist because they require specialized production beyond standard video editing. For content that needs motion graphics or VFX, Hosts should work with a dedicated motion designer.

## Quality Standards & Review Process

Hero Network uses a two-layer quality assurance process:

1. **Internal QA review** — every edit goes through an internal quality check before it reaches the Host. This ensures edits meet Hero Network's standards for technical quality, brand consistency, and brief accuracy.

2. **Host review and revision cycle** — after passing internal QA, the Host reviews the deliverables in the platform and can request revisions if changes are needed.

This dual-review process means Hosts receive edits that have already been vetted for quality, and still have full control to refine the output.

---

## How the Smart Library Powers Better Edits

The Client-level Smart Library is a key differentiator in editing quality and consistency:

- **Brand assets are always available** — editors have access to logos, brand colors, fonts, and style references stored in the Client's Smart Library
- **B-roll persists across projects** — media uploaded to any project is stored at the Client level and available for future projects
- **Consistency across deliverables** — whether it's the first project or the twentieth, editors pull from the same brand asset library, ensuring visual consistency

This means the editing team gets better at producing on-brand content over time, without the Host needing to re-upload assets or re-explain brand guidelines for every project.

## Content Guidelines for Editor Capabilities

- **Be specific about what's included** — "audio cleanup includes background noise reduction and level normalization" beats "professional audio"
- **Don't describe the editing as AI-powered** — position as "professional editing" for now. This will be updated when AI editing features ship.
- **Always mention the Smart Library** when discussing editing quality — it's the reason brand consistency improves over time
- **Never overpromise** — Hero Network does not include motion graphics, animation, or VFX
- **Emphasize the time comparison** — Hosts submit a brief and review a finished product; they don't spend hours editing themselves
- **Reference the dual QA process** — internal review + Host revision cycle is a credibility signal
