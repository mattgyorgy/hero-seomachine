> **Purpose:** Provides the SEO content machine with customer stories, case studies, testimonials, and social proof to power E-E-A-T signals and build credibility in content. Every article should be able to reference specific customer scenarios.

---

## Primary Customer Segments

1. **Agencies** — Marketing, creative, and video agencies producing content for multiple clients
2. **Freelancers** — Independent video producers, content creators, and consultants
3. **In-House Teams** — Internal marketing/content teams at companies producing their own video

---

## The Core Objection: Education & Benefit Gap

The biggest barrier to adoption is not price, quality concerns, or switching costs — it's that prospects **don't fully understand what Hero Network does or why they need it.** Most prospects are stuck in one of these mindsets:

- "I already have a way to record video" (but they're duct-taping Zoom + Dropbox + freelancers)
- "I don't produce enough video to need a platform" (but they would if the friction was lower)
- "Professional remote video requires expensive gear and on-site production" (it doesn't)

Case studies should be framed around the **"aha moment"** — the point where the customer realized what they were missing.

## Content Guidelines for Customer Proof

- **Always frame around the aha moment** — the education gap is the real barrier, not price or quality
- **Show the "before" state honestly** — prospects relate to duct-taped workflows because that's where they are now
- **Use specific numbers** — "4 clients to 12 clients" beats "tripled their capacity"
- **Never fabricate metrics** — use templates marked [MATT:] until real data is available
- **Reference Smart Library** in agency/freelancer stories — cross-project asset reuse is a differentiator they don't know they need
- **Highlight Guest experience** — zero friction for the person being recorded is a massive selling point that prospects undervalue until they see it
