## Core Positioning: Full Replacement, Not a Plugin

Hero Network is **not** another tool in the stack. It's a horizontal platform that replaces the fragmented collection of tools agencies and creators currently duct-tape together for remote video production.

**Do NOT position Hero Network as:**
- "Works alongside your existing tools"
- "Integrates with Frame.io/Dropbox/Slack"
- "Part of your video production stack"

**DO position Hero Network as:**
- "Replaces your entire remote video production workflow"
- "One platform for recording, asset management, editing, and delivery"
- "Stop paying for 4 tools when one does it all"

## What Hero Network Replaces

| Current Tool | What It's Used For | Hero Network Equivalent |
|-------------|-------------------|------------------------|
| **Zoom / Google Meet** | Recording remote video calls | Built-in high-fidelity recorder with mobile browser Guest access |
| **Dropbox / Google Drive** | Storing and sharing raw footage and assets | Smart Library (Client-level DAM) with automatic asset organization |

| **File transfer services** (WeTransfer, Dropbox links) | Sending large video files | Automatic chunk upload during recording — no manual file transfers |

### The "Before" Stack (What Prospects Use Today)

A typical agency producing remote video content currently uses 4–6 tools:

1. **Zoom** — $13-22/mo per host for recording
2. **Dropbox or Google Drive** — $10-20/mo for storage and file sharing
3. **Frame.io or similar** — $15-25/mo for review workflows
4. **Freelance editor** — $300-500 per project
5. **Email/Slack** — free, but hours of coordination time
6. **Project management** (Asana, Trello, etc.) — $10-25/mo for tracking

**Monthly cost for 5 videos/month:** $548–$2,592/mo (tools + freelance editing) plus 5-10 hours/month of coordination time

### The "After" Stack (Hero Network)

1. **Hero Network** — $79/mo + $99/project for editing

**Monthly cost for 5 videos/month:** $574/mo, zero coordination overhead

The cost is comparable or lower, but the time savings are massive because everything happens in one platform with no file transfers, no editor sourcing, and no tool-switching.

## Competitive Positioning by Replacement Category

### vs. "Zoom + Freelance Editor" Workflow

This is the most common setup Hero Network replaces.

**Pain points to highlight:**
- Zoom recordings are compressed and low quality — Hero Network captures high-fidelity video via the Guest's mobile camera
- File transfer is manual — someone has to download from Zoom, upload to Dropbox, share with the editor
- Editor has no context — they receive raw files with no brand assets, no prior work to reference
- Every project starts from scratch — no persistent asset library, no accumulated brand knowledge

**Hero Network advantage:** Recording, asset management, and editing submission happen in one platform. The Smart Library means editors always have brand context. No file transfers, no coordination.

### vs. "DIY Editing" Workflow (CapCut, Descript, etc.)

For prospects who edit their own videos.

**Pain points to highlight:**
- Hours spent editing per video instead of producing more content
- Editing skill ceiling — most people can make "okay" edits, not professional ones
- No quality assurance — no second set of eyes before delivery
- Time is the real cost — an agency owner's time is worth more than $99/video

**Hero Network advantage:** Submit a brief, get back professional edits. The Host's time goes into the creative and client relationship, not into timeline scrubbing.

### vs. "Full Production Company" Approach

For in-house teams outsourcing to video production agencies.

**Pain points to highlight:**
- $1,000–5,000+ per video for on-site production
- 2-4 week timelines per project
- Requires scheduling, location, equipment, crew
- Doesn't scale — every video has the same fixed cost

**Hero Network advantage:** Remote recording eliminates the need for on-site production. $79/mo + $99/project is a fraction of the cost. 3-5 day turnaround. Scales linearly — 1 video or 50 videos, same workflow.

## Content Guidelines

- **Lead with replacement, not integration** — Hero Network replaces tools, it doesn't plug into them
- **Name the specific tools being replaced** — "Replace Zoom + Dropbox + freelance editors" is more compelling than "streamline your workflow"
- **Show the cost math** — compare the full cost of the current stack (tools + time + editing) against Hero Network's flat pricing
- **Emphasize time savings alongside cost** — the biggest win is often the hours eliminated, not just the dollars saved
- **Don't claim integrations that don't exist** — no Zapier, no API, no direct publishing yet
- **Position download-only delivery as simple, not limited** — "Download your finished edits" is clean and clear
