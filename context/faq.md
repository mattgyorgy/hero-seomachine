## Top Misconception: "It's Just Another Zoom Recorder"

This is the #1 misconception the SEO machine must correct in content. Hero Network is not a recording plugin, screen recorder, or Zoom add-on.

**What people assume:** Hero Network is a tool that records video calls, similar to Riverside, Squadcast, or Zoom's recording feature.

**What Hero Network actually is:** A complete remote video production platform — recording, Client-level asset management (Smart Library), structured editing briefs, professional editing ($99/project), internal QA, in-platform review, and final delivery. It replaces the entire fragmented workflow (Zoom + Dropbox + freelance editors + project management).

**How to correct this in content:**
- Never describe Hero Network as a "recording tool" or "recording platform" alone
- Always mention the full workflow: recording → Smart Library → editing → delivery
- Compare the complete platform against the fragmented stack, not against other recorders
- Use language like "end-to-end remote video production platform" instead of "video recorder"

## Frequently Asked Questions

### Recording & Quality

**Q: Can mobile phone video really look professional?**

The remote recording captures high-fidelity video through the Guest's mobile browser camera. It's not DSLR quality — and that's a feature, not a limitation. For remote interviews, testimonials, thought leadership, and agency client content, mobile capture provides more than enough quality. The professional polish comes from the editing pipeline (cuts, pacing, audio cleanup, color correction, captions, graphics, B-roll insertion), not from the camera hardware. Most viewers can't tell the difference between a well-edited mobile recording and a DSLR shoot when the editing is done right.

**Q: Does the Guest need to install anything?**

No. Guests receive a link via email or SMS, open it in their mobile browser, and join the live recording session. No app download, no account creation, no technical setup. This is intentional — zero friction for Guests means higher completion rates and a better experience for everyone.

**Q: Does the Host need special equipment?**

No. The Host uses the platform on their desktop with their standard webcam and microphone. No special hardware required. Just a modern browser and an internet connection.

**Q: What about internet quality? What if the connection drops?**

Hero Network uses packeted chunk uploads — video is uploaded in small segments during the recording session, not as a single large file afterward. This means recordings are resilient to brief connection issues. The footage is stitched together in AWS and delivered to the project automatically.

### Editing

**Q: How much control do I have over the edit?**

Full control. Hosts submit a structured editing brief (style preferences, deliverable types, target length) plus free-form notes with specific creative direction. After the edit is complete and passes internal QA, the Host reviews the deliverables in the platform and can request revisions.

**Q: What if I don't like the edit?**

The revision cycle exists for this reason. Hosts review the edit in the platform and submit revision notes for any changes. The editing team revises and redelivers.

**Q: What's included in the $99 per project editing fee?**

All deliverables for that project — full episode, social clips, vertical shorts, whatever the Host requests. It's not per-clip or per-format. One project, one fee, all deliverables included. Editing includes cuts/pacing, transitions, audio cleanup, color correction, captions, lower thirds/graphics, and B-roll insertion from the Smart Library.

**Q: What's NOT included in editing?**

Custom motion graphics, animation, and visual effects (VFX). These require specialized production capabilities beyond standard video editing.

**Q: How long does editing take?**

Standard turnaround is 3–5 business days. Rush turnaround is available at additional cost.

### Platform & Workflow

**Q: Can I manage multiple clients?**

Yes — unlimited clients and unlimited projects are included in the $79/mo plan. Clients are the top-level organizational unit, and each Client has their own Smart Library (DAM) for persistent asset management across projects.

**Q: What is the Smart Library?**

The Smart Library is Hero Network's Client-level Digital Asset Management system. Any media recorded or uploaded to a project is stored in the Client's Smart Library and available for reuse across all future projects for that Client. This means brand assets (logos, fonts, B-roll) are always accessible to editors without re-uploading.

**Q: Can I save Guests for reuse?**

Yes. Guests are saved in the platform and can be reused across projects. You don't need to re-enter their information for each new recording session.

### Pricing

**Q: What does $79/month include?**

10 hours of recording per month, 250GB of storage, unlimited projects, unlimited clients, Smart Library, scheduling/calendar invites, Guest link invitations, status tracking, and in-platform review. Editing is an additional $99 per project.

**Q: Is there a free trial?**

Yes. Hero Network offers a 14-day free trial. After completing the onboarding wizard, new Hosts also receive a $99 video editing credit at no cost — enough to get one project professionally edited for free. This means you can try the full workflow (recording → Smart Library → professional editing → delivery) before paying anything.

**Q: What happens if I exceed my recording hours or storage?**

You'll need to upgrade to a higher-tier plan. Higher-tier plans with increased limits are coming. There are no overage charges — it's a hard cap, not a metered billing model.

**Q: What's Founder Pricing?**

Founder Pricing is $79/month, locked in forever for early adopters. This price will not increase for Hosts who sign up during the Founder Pricing period. Future pricing for new sign-ups may be higher.

### Technical

**Q: What devices and browsers are supported?**

Hosts use the platform on desktop with any modern browser. Guests record using their mobile browser — no app required. The only requirement is a stable internet connection.

**Q: What file format are edits delivered in?**

MP4 is the standard delivery format.

**Q: Can I publish directly to YouTube or social platforms?**

Not yet — currently Hosts download finished edits from the platform. Direct publishing to social platforms is in development.

### Onboarding & Support

**Q: How do I get started?**

New Hosts go through a guided onboarding flow: create their first Client, upload brand assets to the Smart Library, invite team members, and create their first Project. The onboarding is hands-on — not just a help article.

## Content Guidelines

- **Always correct the "Zoom recorder" misconception** — this is the most common and most damaging misunderstanding
- **Frame mobile recording quality as intentional** — "It's not DSLR quality, and that's a feature" is the right tone
- **Acknowledge limitations honestly** — no motion graphics, no direct publishing yet, 10hr/250GB caps. Honesty builds trust.
- **Use the education gap framing** — most prospects don't know what they're missing, so content should illuminate the problem before presenting the solution
- **Never be defensive** — address objections matter-of-factly, not with excessive justification
